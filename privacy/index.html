Privacy Policy

How we handle information you share with Gerlach Law, PLLC through this website. Last updated April 27, 2026.

What this policy covers

This policy applies to information collected through gerlachlegal.com and its subpages, operated by Gerlach Law, PLLC ("the firm," "we," "us"). It does not cover information collected in the course of an existing client engagement, which is governed by the firm's engagement letter and the Rules Regulating The Florida Bar.

Information we collect

The site is mostly static. The only information we actively collect is what you submit through the contact form: your name, email address, phone number (if provided), and the message you write. Standard server-side access logs may record IP address, browser type, and request timestamps for routine site-operations purposes.

How we treat contact-form communications

Information submitted through the contact form is treated as a communication from a prospective client. Under Rule 4-1.18 of the Rules Regulating The Florida Bar, the firm may not reveal information learned in such consultations except as the rule permits, even if the firm does not end up representing you. However:

  • Submitting a form does not, by itself, create an attorney-client relationship.
  • The communication is not privileged in the formal sense until an engagement letter is signed.
  • If you and the firm later have a conflict that prevents representation, Rule 4-1.18 limits how the firm may use your information but does not prohibit all use.
  • Do not send detailed or sensitive information about an active matter through the form. Wait until we have confirmed there is no conflict and you have a signed engagement letter.

How we use the information

Form submissions are used to evaluate whether the firm can take on the matter, schedule an initial consultation, and follow up by email or phone. Server logs are used for site-operations purposes (uptime, troubleshooting, security). We do not sell, rent, or trade your information to third parties. We do not share your information with marketing or advertising vendors.

Cookies and tracking

The site is built as a static website and does not set first-party cookies for tracking, advertising, or analytics by default. If we add analytics or scheduling tools in the future, this policy will be updated to describe what they collect.

Third-party services

The site loads web fonts from Google Fonts (fonts.googleapis.com, fonts.gstatic.com). When the site is hosted on GitHub Pages, GitHub may collect basic request metadata as described in its own privacy policy. If you click through to a third-party scheduling tool, calendar service, or court-record link from this site, that third party's privacy practices apply once you leave gerlachlegal.com.

Your choices

You can email assistant@gerlachlegal.com to ask what information the firm has about you, request correction of inaccurate information, or request deletion of a contact-form submission that did not lead to representation. If applicable law (such as the California Consumer Privacy Act) gives you additional rights and you are a resident of that jurisdiction, the firm will honor those rights to the extent they apply.

Children

The site is not directed at children under 13, and the firm does not knowingly collect information from them through this site.

Security

The site is served over HTTPS. Form submissions are transmitted over an encrypted connection and stored in the firm's case-management system. No system is perfectly secure; do not transmit sensitive information through any web form unless you are willing to accept the residual risk of online communication.

Changes to this policy

If the firm changes how it handles information collected through the site, this page will be updated and the "last updated" date at the top will change. Material changes will be reflected here before they take effect.
