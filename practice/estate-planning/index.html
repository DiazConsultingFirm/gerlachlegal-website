03 · Estate Planning

Wills, trusts, and probate that protect what you've built.

Florida estate planning and probate under Fla. Stat. ch. 731-735, with homestead, durable POA, and healthcare-directive coverage built around your family — not a generic template.

What we handle

  • Wills — drafted to Florida's § 732.502 execution requirements (writing, testator signature at the end, two attesting witnesses).
  • Revocable and irrevocable trusts — probate avoidance, asset protection, tax planning, Medicaid planning.
  • Probate and estate administration — formal administration and summary administration under Fla. Stat. ch. 735 for estates of $75,000 or less in non-exempt assets or where the decedent has been deceased more than two years.
  • Powers of attorney — durable POAs under Fla. Stat. ch. 709, drafted to take effect immediately on signing (Florida no longer recognizes "springing" POAs signed after October 1, 2011).
  • Healthcare surrogate & living will — designation under Fla. Stat. ch. 765, paired with a clear advance directive.
  • Lady Bird deeds — enhanced life estate deeds for probate avoidance and Medicaid estate-recovery planning.
  • Business succession planning — buy-sell agreements, life-insurance funding, trusts, and family-transition structures so the business survives the founder.

Common questions

Do I need a will in Florida?

+

If you die without a will, your assets pass under the intestate succession statute (Fla. Stat. ch. 732), which may not match your wishes. A will lets you choose your personal representative, name guardians for minor children, and direct who receives your property.

Revocable vs irrevocable trust?

+

A revocable trust can be amended or revoked during the grantor's life and is commonly used for probate avoidance. An irrevocable trust generally cannot be changed once funded; it is used for asset protection, tax planning, and Medicaid planning.

How does Florida homestead protection work?

+

Florida's constitutional homestead protection shields the primary residence from most creditor claims, caps property tax increases through Save Our Homes, and limits the owner's ability to devise homestead when survived by a spouse or minor child.

What is a Lady Bird deed?

+

A Lady Bird deed (enhanced life estate deed) lets the property owner keep full lifetime control — including the right to sell or refinance — and pass the property at death without probate.
