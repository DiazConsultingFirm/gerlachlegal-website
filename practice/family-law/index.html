01 · Family Law

Florida family law, practiced like family matters.

Divorce, custody and time-sharing, support, paternity, adoption, prenups, post-judgment modification — handled under Fla. Stat. ch. 61 with case-plan tracking from intake to final judgment.

What we handle

Family cases come in fast and don't wait for a clean schedule. The list below covers most of what walks through the door, but if your situation isn't here, ask during the consult — we'll tell you whether we're the right fit before either of us is committed.

  • Divorce and legal separation — uncontested, contested, simplified, and high-asset. Includes equitable distribution, alimony, and parenting plans.
  • Paternity, child custody, and visitation — establishing paternity, time-sharing schedules, parental responsibility, and parenting plan disputes under Fla. Stat. § 61.13.
  • Child support — initial determinations under the Florida child support guidelines (Fla. Stat. § 61.30), enforcement, and modification.
  • Adoption — step-parent, relative, and agency-facilitated adoptions under Fla. Stat. ch. 63.
  • Post-judgment modification, enforcement, and relocation — substantial-change petitions, contempt, wage garnishment, and Fla. Stat. § 61.13001 relocation matters.
  • Temporary custody by relative or family member — Fla. Stat. ch. 751 petitions when both parents consent or are unable to care for the child.
  • Postnuptial and prenuptial agreements — drafted and reviewed under the Florida Uniform Premarital Agreement Act, Fla. Stat. § 61.079.

Common questions

Is Florida a no-fault divorce state?

+

Yes. Florida is a pure no-fault divorce state under Fla. Stat. § 61.052. To file, you only need to state the marriage is irretrievably broken.

How does Florida decide custody and time-sharing?

+

Time-sharing is decided under Fla. Stat. § 61.13 on the best interests of the child standard. Florida law presumes equal time-sharing is in the child's best interests unless rebutted by evidence on the statutory factors.

Can I relocate with my child after divorce?

+

Florida's relocation statute (§ 61.13001) requires written consent of every other parent or court approval before any move of more than 50 miles for 60 consecutive days or more.

Are prenups enforceable in Florida?

+

Yes, under the Florida Uniform Premarital Agreement Act (§ 61.079), if the agreement is in writing, signed voluntarily, and based on fair financial disclosure or a valid waiver.
