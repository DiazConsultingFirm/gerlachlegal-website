04 · Civil Litigation

Contract, property, and construction disputes — resolved with the result in mind.

Florida civil litigation in state court, including pre-suit demand letters, mediation, summary judgment, trial, and appeal. We don't bill the case to death — we get to a result.

What we handle

  • Contract disputes — breach claims, declaratory judgment actions, anticipatory repudiation, and proposals for settlement under Fla. Stat. § 768.79.
  • Property disputes — boundary, easement, quiet title (Fla. Stat. ch. 65), and partition actions involving real property anywhere in Florida.
  • Construction litigation — Fla. Stat. ch. 558 pre-suit notice, ch. 713 construction lien claims and defenses, and statute-of-repose disputes.
  • Business and partnership disputes — LLC member and partnership disagreements under Fla. Stat. ch. 605 (Florida Revised LLC Act) and ch. 620; buy-sell enforcement, derivative claims, and dissolution.
  • Collections and post-judgment enforcement — writs of execution and garnishment under Fla. Stat. ch. 56, proceedings supplementary, and judgment-lien renewals to keep a judgment collectible.
  • Civil appeals from final judgments — appellate review under Fla. R. App. P. 9.110, including 30-day notice deadlines and tolling motions under Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.530.

How we work the case

Most civil cases settle. Some go to trial. We treat both as outcomes worth preparing for from day one. Demand letter goes out, mediation is calendared, summary judgment papers are drafted in the background — so when the other side blinks, we're ready, and when they don't, the file is trial-ready by the deadline. Florida adopted the federal summary-judgment standard in 2021, which means motion practice is more decisive than it used to be; we plan around that.

Common questions

Do I need to send a demand letter before suing?

+

It depends on the claim. Some Florida statutes require pre-suit notice — for example, Fla. Stat. ch. 558 (construction defect), § 768.28 (claims against the State), and certain insurance and FDUTPA claims. Most ordinary contract and tort claims do not require a demand letter, but one is often strategically valuable.

How does construction litigation differ?

+

Florida's Chapter 558 imposes a mandatory pre-suit notice and opportunity-to-cure process before a construction-defect lawsuit. Construction claims also intersect with lien rights under Fla. Stat. ch. 713 and statutes of repose, all of which create deadlines well before any traditional limitations clock runs.

Can I recover attorney's fees?

+

Florida follows the "American Rule" — each side pays its own fees — unless a contract or statute provides otherwise. However, a proposal for settlement under Fla. Stat. § 768.79 can shift fees if the proposal is not accepted and the result is sufficiently better than the offer.

Is mediation required before trial?

+

Most Florida civil divisions require mediation under Fla. Stat. ch. 44 and the local case-management orders. Mediation is confidential, the mediator does not decide the case, and a settlement is binding only if reduced to writing and signed.
