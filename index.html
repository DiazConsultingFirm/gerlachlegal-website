Straight answers

Florida law questions, answered.

The questions we get most often at consultations, in plain English. If you don't see yours here, ask during your consultation.

Is Florida a no-fault divorce state?

+

Yes. Florida is a pure no-fault divorce state under Fla. Stat. § 61.052. To file, you only need to state the marriage is irretrievably broken.

How does Florida decide child custody and time-sharing?

+

Custody is decided as time-sharing under Fla. Stat. § 61.13 using the best interests of the child standard.

What is a shelter hearing?

+

A shelter hearing is the first court hearing in a Florida dependency case after a child is removed from their home. Under Fla. Stat. § 39.402, it generally occurs within 24 hours of removal.

Do I need a will in Florida?

+

If you die without a will in Florida, your assets pass under the intestate succession statute (Fla. Stat. ch. 732), which may not match your wishes.

How long do I have to appeal a civil final judgment?

+

A notice of appeal must be filed within 30 days of rendition of the final order under Fla. R. App. P. 9.110.
