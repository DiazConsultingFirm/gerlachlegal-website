Meet your attorney

Florida Bar since 2016. 2020 Elite Lawyer. Founder of Gerlach Law, PLLC — a Fort Lauderdale firm built so families get steady representation through Family Law, Juvenile Dependency, Estate Planning, and Civil Litigation.

Angela Elaine Gerlach, Esq.

Angela E. Gerlach has been admitted to the Florida Bar since 2016 and, that same year, founded Gerlach Law, PLLC, in Fort Lauderdale, Florida. She has been representing Clients in Family Law, Juvenile Dependency, Estate Planning, and Civil Litigation matters across Florida.

Angela E. Gerlach is a Florida family law attorney whose practice is dedicated to safeguarding children and preserving family stability. A graduate of Nova Southeastern University Shepard Broad College of Law, she concentrates in family law and juvenile dependency, with experience in custody, adoption, child protection, and complex divorce matters. Recognized for her thoughtful counsel, meticulous preparation, and fierce advocacy in the courtroom in every case she handles. She delivers tailored legal solutions and effective courtroom representation in matters of the highest personal and legal consequences.

She takes cases from anywhere in the state. Most clients arrive during the worst week of a particular problem: a dependency hearing they did not see coming, a contested divorce that has stopped being workable, a parent's estate with no will, a civil matter with a deadline. The first conversation is free. It is used to figure out whether the firm can actually help, what the work would cost in plain numbers, and whether the timing fits before any retainer is signed.

Angela was recognized as a 2020 Elite Lawyer in family law. The Rules Regulating The Florida Bar (Rule 4-7) make clear that past recognition does not predict outcomes, and she would rather be measured on the next case than the last award. If you are trying to figure out whether this is the right firm for what you are going through, the consultation line is open. The intake form on the contact page is the fastest path to a return call.
